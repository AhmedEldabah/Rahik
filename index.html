My Rahik,

I started this letter four times before I let myself keep going. Every version sounded too backflippy — like I was trying to impress you instead of just talking to you.

So here's the honest one. I think about you at the strangest moments — when I'm waiting for something to load, when I'm pooping, when I'm eating seafood, right now. When I notice the sky doing something nice and my first thought is that you'd like this. You've quietly become the person I narrate my day to, even in my head.

I love how you laugh at everything I say. I love that you take small things seriously and big things also seriously — which is funny, like hey, do you take anything lightly? I love the way you argue with me, actually argue, talk to me, laugh with me. Soon, beat me. I love doing anything with you.

There is no point to this letter. I just wanted to do something nice for you, because I know how much you've been stressing.

All I wanted to say at the end is — Rahik, I love you, and I can't wait to spend forever with you.

Yours, completely —
Ahmed
